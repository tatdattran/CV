Teaching in SS2020
In summer semester 2020, I offer the following courses:



2SWS Lecture: Introduction to Combinatorics.
Time and Place: Tuesdays, 09:15 – 10:45, P-701 ( started from 07.04.2020)
Register: Via moodle or Email: tran@math.uni-leipzig.de

Content:
  • How many possible sudoku puzzles are there?
  • In a lottery where six balls are selected from forty-nine, how often do two winning balls have consecutive numbers?
  • In how many ways can we give change for €1 using only 10cent, 20cent, and 50cent pieces?
  • Can we tile a floor with squares and regular hexagons?
  • In chess, can a knight visit all the sixty-four squares of a chessboard just once and return to its starting point?
Combinatorics, the branch of mathematics concerned with selecting, arranging, and counting collections of objects, works to answer such these questions. In this course, I will give out a short introduction to this beautiful subject. This course is designed to be accessible for the 3rd year students and higher.

Seminar: Combinatorics and its applications
Time and Place: Fridays, 09:15-10:45, SG 2-14 (started from 17.04.2020)
Register: Via moodle or Email: tran@math.uni-leipzig.de

Aim:
This reading seminar goes along with my lecture: Introduction to Combinatorics. The aim is to create a platform for students so that they can practice their skill in reading and presenting mathematical problems.

Organization:
Topics will be chosen mainly from some chapters (from chapter 28 to chapter 45) in the book “Proofs from The Book” of Aigner, Martin and Ziegler, Günter M. (The E-book version of this book has been bought by Leipzig University, students can download it from the website of UL-Bibliothek, for more details can see in Moodle).
Further topics will be updated depend on the progress.
Each participant is expected to choose a chapter (4-10 pages) for her/his presentation.
