Teaching in WS19/20
In winter semester 2019/2020, I will give the following course (it will be in English and intended to students from the 5th semester):



4SWS Vorlesung: An introduction to Geometric and Algebraic Methods in Statistics.
Tuesdays 09:15-10:45 at SG 3-10
and
Thursdays 15:15-16:45 at SG 3-10
Abstract:
This lecture consists of two main parts: In the first part, students will learn some core concepts in information geometry (a differential geometry approach in Statistics) such as: Fisher information metrics, Wright manifolds, free energy functional, entropy, exponential family, mixture family, etc. The second part will be some core concepts in algebraic statistics (has advances when the statistical model is not regular and can not be approached by differential geometry methods) such as: Markov bases, likelihood inference, conditional independence, hidden variables, etc.

Refs:
[1]. J. Hofrichter, J. Jost, T. D. Tran, Information Geometry and Population Genetics: The mathematical structure of the Wright-Fisher model, Springer, 2017.
[2]. M. Drton, B. Sturmfels, S. Sullivant, Lectures on Algebraic Statistics, 2009.
[3]. ... updated during the course.





2SWS Rechnerpraktikum: Wednesdays 09:15-10:45 at computer lab A 312
Abstract:
In this practice course, students will learn some basic knowledge about two popular languages in Statistics (Python and R), how to apply it into problems in the Vorlesung and if possible, some real problems such as cancer diagnose.


2SWS Machine learning practice: Thursday 09:15-10:45 at computer lab A 312
Abstract:
Students learn the basic knowledge about R so that they can really understand and write codes by themselves in problems in machine learning.
